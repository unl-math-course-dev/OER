We solve \(P = 12 + 2t^3\) for \(t\) in terms of \(P\text{.}\) \begin{align*} 2t^3 \amp = P - 12\amp\amp\text{Substract 12 from both sides.}\\ t^3 \amp = \frac{P - 12}{2}\amp\amp\text{Divide both sides by 2.}\\ t \amp = \sqrt[3]{\frac{P - 12}{2}}\amp\amp\text{Take cube roots.} \end{align*} The inverse function is \(t = f^{-1}(P) =\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{P - 12}{2}}\text{.}\) It tells us the number of years it takes for the pheasant population to grow to size \(P\text{.}\)
