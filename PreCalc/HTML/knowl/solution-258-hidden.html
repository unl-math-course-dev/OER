Next, we make a table showing \(x\)-values close to \(3\text{,}\) as in Figure 335a. As we choose \(x\)-values closer and closer to \(3\text{,}\) \((x - 3)^2\) gets closer to \(0\text{,}\) so the fraction \(\dfrac{2}{(x-3)^2}\) gets very large. This means that the graph approaches, but never touches, the vertical line \(x=3\text{.}\) In other words, the graph has a vertical asymptote at \(x=3\text{.}\) We indicate the vertical asymptote by a dashed line, as shown in Figure 335b.
