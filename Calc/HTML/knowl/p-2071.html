In FigureÂ 2.32, we saw an interesting relationship between the slopes of tangent lines to the natural exponential and natural logarithm functions at points reflected across the line \(y = x\text{.}\) In particular, we observed that at the point \((\ln(2), 2)\) on the graph of \(f(x) = e^x\text{,}\) the slope of the tangent line is \(f'(\ln(2)) = 2\text{,}\) while at the corresponding point \((2, \ln(2))\) on the graph of \(f^{-1}(x) = \ln(x)\text{,}\) the slope of the tangent line is \((f^{-1})'(2) = \frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) which is the reciprocal of \(f'(\ln(2))\text{.}\)
