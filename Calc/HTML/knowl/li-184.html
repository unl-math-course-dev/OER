Item1.2.7.b

\(y = g(x)\) such that

  • \(g(-2) = 3\text{,}\) \(g(-1) = -1\text{,}\) \(g(1) = -2\text{,}\) and \(g(2) = 3\)
  • At \(x = -2, -1, 1\) and \(2\text{,}\) \(g\) has a limit, and its limit equals the value of the function at that point.
  • \(g(0)\) is not defined and \(\lim_{x \to 0} g(x)\) does not exist.
