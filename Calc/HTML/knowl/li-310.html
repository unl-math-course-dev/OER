Item1.3.7.a

For this problem, you should draw a graph so that the slope of the secant line from \((-3,f(-3))\) to \((0,f(0))\) is \(-2\text{,}\) and similarly the slope of the secant line from \((1,f(1))\) to \((3,f(3))\) is \(0.5\text{,}\) while the slopes of the tangent lines at \(x = -1\) and \(x = 2\) are \(-1\) and \(1\text{,}\) respectively. For instance, you could let \(f(-3) = 3\) and have \(f\) pass through the points \((-3,3)\text{,}\) \((-1,-2)\text{,}\) \((0,-3)\text{,}\) \((1,-2)\text{,}\) and \((3,-1)\) and draw the desired tangent lines accordingly.
