  1. Given \(p(x) = x^3 - a^2x\) on \([0,a]\text{,}\) we first find the critical number(s) of \(p\) that lie in \([0,a]\text{.}\) Since \(p'(x) = 3x^2 - a^2 = 3\left(x^2 - \frac{a^2}{3} \right)\text{,}\) we know \(p\) has critical numbers at \(x = \pm \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) The negative critical number lies outside the interval, while the positive one lies within the interval since \(0 \lt \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \lt 1\text{.}\) Evaluating \(p\) at the endpoints and the critical number within the interval, we find that \(p(0) = 0\text{,}\) \(p(a) = a^3 - a^2 \cdot a = 0\text{,}\) and \(p\left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = \left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right)^3 - a^2 \cdot \left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = \frac{a^3}{3\sqrt{3}} - \frac{a^3}{\sqrt{3}} = -\frac{2a^3}{3\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) Hence, the global maximum of \(p\) on \([0,a]\) is \(p(0) = p(a) = 0\) and the global minimum is \(p\left( \frac{a}{\sqrt{3}} \right) = -\frac{2a^3}{3\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\)

  2. Given \(r(x) = axe^{-bx}\) on \(\left[\frac{1}{2b}, \frac{2}{b}\right]\text{,}\) we first find the critical numbers of \(r\) that lie in the given interval. Note that by the product rule, \(r'(x) = ax e^{-bx} (-b) + e^{-bx} a\text{.}\) Factoring, \(r'(x) = ae^{-bx}(-bx + 1)\text{,}\) and thus the only critical number of \(r\) occurs where \(x = \frac{1}{b}\text{.}\) Note that \(\frac{1}{2b} \lt \frac{1}{b} \lt b\) since \(b \gt 1\text{,}\) and thus the critical number lies within the interval.

    Evaluating \(r\) at the endpoints and the critical number, we find

    • \(r\left( \frac{1}{2b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{1}{2b} \cdot e^{-1/2} \approx 0.607 \frac{a}{2b} = 0.3035 \frac{a}{b}\)

    • \(r\left( \frac{2}{b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{2}{b} \cdot e^{-2} \approx 0.135 \frac{2a}{b} = 0.270 \frac{a}{b}\)

    • \(r\left( \frac{1}{b} \right) = a \cdot \frac{1}{b} \cdot e^{-1} \approx 0.368 \frac{a}{b} \)

    The global max of \(r\) is therefore \(r\left( \frac{1}{b} \right) \approx 0.368 \frac{a}{b}\) and the global min is \(r\left( \frac{2}{b} \right) \approx 0.270 \frac{a}{b}\text{.}\)

  3. For \(w(x) = a(1-e^{-bx})\) on \([b, 3b]\text{,}\) we first note that \(w'(x) = a(be^{-bx}) = abe^{-bx}\text{.}\) Since \(a\) and \(b\) are both positive and so is \(e^{-bx}\) for every value of \(x\text{,}\) we see that \(w'(x) \gt 0\) for every value of \(x\text{.}\) Because \(w\) is always increasing (specifically on \([b,3b]\)), \(w\) has its global minimum at the left endpoint (\(g(b) = a(1-e^{-b^2})\)) and its global maximum at the right endpoint (\(g(3b) = a(1-e^{-3b^2})\)).

  4. Finally, for \(s(x) = \sin(kx)\) on \(\left[\frac{\pi}{3k}, \frac{5\pi}{6k}\right]\text{,}\) we know \(s'(x) = k\cos(kx)\text{,}\) and \(\cos(kx) = 0\) when \(kx = \frac{\pi}{2} \pm n\pi\) for some integer \(n\text{,}\) so \(x = \frac{\pi}{2k} \pm n\pi = \frac{\pi \pm 2n\pi}{2k}\text{.}\) These \(x\)-values are the critical numbers of \(s\text{.}\) Note that when \(n = -1, 0, 1\text{,}\) the corresponding critical numbers are

    \begin{equation*} x = -\frac{\pi}{2k}, \frac{\pi}{2k}, \frac{3\pi}{2k} \end{equation*}

    Comparing the values of \(-\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) \(\frac{\pi}{2k}\text{,}\) and \(\frac{3\pi}{2k}\) to \(\frac{\pi}{3}\) and \(\frac{5\pi}{6}\text{,}\) we see that only \(\frac{\pi}{2k}\) lies in the given interval \(\left[\frac{\pi}{3k}, \frac{5\pi}{6k}\right]\text{,}\) and thus \(x = \frac{\pi}{2k}\) is the only critical number at which we need to evaluate \(s\text{.}\)

    Evaluating \(s\) at the endpoints and relevant critical number, we find:

    • \(s\left( \frac{\pi}{3k} \right) = \sin\left( k \cdot \frac{\pi}{3k} \right) = \sin\left( \frac{\pi}{3} \right) = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\text{;}\)

    • \(s\left( \frac{5\pi}{6k} \right) = \sin\left( k \cdot \frac{5\pi}{6k} \right) = \sin\left( \frac{5\pi}{6} \right) = \frac{1}{2}\text{;}\) and

    • \(s\left( \frac{\pi}{2k} \right) = \sin\left( k \cdot \frac{\pi}{2k} \right) = \sin\left( \frac{\pi}{2} \right) = 1\text{.}\)

    Hence the global max of \(s\) on \(\left[\frac{\pi}{3k}, \frac{5\pi}{6k}\right]\) is \(s\left( \frac{\pi}{2k} \right) = 1\) and the global min of \(s\) is \(s\left( \frac{5\pi}{6k} \right) = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
