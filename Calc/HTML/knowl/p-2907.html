The fact that \(f'(x) \leq 0\) for all \(x\) in \([a,b]\) means that \(f\) cannot increase in this interval. So either \(f\) is constant or \(f\) decreases somewhere in this interval. We can conclude that the global maximum value of \(f\) occurs at \(x=a\) and the global minimum value at \(x=b\text{.}\)
