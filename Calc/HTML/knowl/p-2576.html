Since \(p(2) = \frac{12}{e^2}\) and \(p'(2) = -\frac{5}{e^2}\text{,}\) the equation of the tangent line to \(p\) at \(x = 2\) is given by \(y = -\frac{5}{e^2}(x-2) + \frac{12}{e^2}\text{.}\) To determine whether the tangent line lies above or below the graph of \(y=p(x)\) at \(x = 2\text{,}\) we consider the concavity of \(p\text{.}\) Recall we know that \(p''(2) = 0\) and that \(p\) is concave down to the left of \(x = 2\) and concave up to the right. Thus, the tangent line lies neither solely below nor solely above the graph of \(y = p(x)\) at \(x = 2\text{;}\) it lies below \(y = p(x)\) to the right of \(x = 2\) and above \(y = p(x)\) to the left of \(x = 2\text{.}\)
