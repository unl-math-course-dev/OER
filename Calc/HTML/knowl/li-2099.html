Item3.3.4.d

On the interval \([\frac{\pi}{3}, \frac{5\pi}{6}] = [\frac{4\pi}{12}, \frac{10\pi}{12}]\text{,}\) only the critical number \(t = \frac{5\pi}{12}\) lies in the interval. Thus we have three values at which to evaluate \(s\text{.}\)

Doing so:

  • \(s(\frac{5\pi}{12}) = 8\text{;}\)

  • \(s(\frac{\pi}{3}) = 3\sin(2(\frac{\pi}{3}-\frac{\pi}{6})) + 5 = 3\sin(\frac{\pi}{6}) + 5 = \frac{3}{2} + 5 = \frac{13}{2} = 6.5\text{;}\) and

  • \(s(\frac{5\pi}{6}) = 3\sin(2(\frac{5\pi}{6}-\frac{\pi}{6})) + 5 = 3\sin(\frac{2\pi}{3}) + 5 = 5 + \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 7.598\text{.}\)

The global max of \(s\) on \([\frac{\pi}{3}, \frac{5\pi}{6}]\) is thus \(s(\frac{5\pi}{12}) = 8\text{,}\) while the global min is \(s(\frac{\pi}{3}) = 6.5\text{.}\)
