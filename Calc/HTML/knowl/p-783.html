In Preview Activity 1.23, the function \(f\) given in Figure 1.59 fails to have a limit at only two values: at \(a = -2\) (where the left- and right-hand limits are 2 and \(-1\text{,}\) respectively) and at \(x = 2\text{,}\) where \(\lim_{x \to 2^+} f(x)\) does not exist). Note well that even at values such as \(a = -1\) and \(a = 0\) where there are holes in the graph, the limit still exists.
