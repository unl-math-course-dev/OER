Item4.3.9.a

The sum property of the definite integral tells us that

\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 [f(x) + g(x)] \, dx = \int_0^1 f(x) \, dx + \int_0^1 g(x) \, dx \end{equation*}

Now \(\int_0^1 f(x) \, dx\) is the area of a triangle of height \(2\) and base \(1\text{,}\) so \(\int_0^1 f(x) \, dx = 1\text{.}\) Also, \(\int_0^1 g(x) \,dx\) is the opposite of the area of a quarter circle of radius \(1\text{,}\) so \(\int_0^1 g(x) \,dx = \frac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\) Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \int_0^1 [f(x) + g(x)] \,dx = \int_0^1 f(x) \,dx + \int_0^1 g(x) \,dx = 1-\frac{\pi}{4}\text{.} \end{equation*}
