Item3.1.7.b

If we compute \(p''\left(\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2} \right)\text{,}\) we find that \(p''\left(\frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2} \right) \approx -1.205\text{,}\) which means that \(p\) is concave down at \(x = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}\text{.}\) Since \(p\) has a horizontal tangent line at this point, it follows that \(p\) has a local maximum at \(x = \frac{\sqrt{5}-1}{2}\) by the second derivative test.
