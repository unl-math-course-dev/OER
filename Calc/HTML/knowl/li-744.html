Item1.7.8.a

As seen at left in the figure below in part (d), \(g\) has a sharp corner point at \(x = 0\text{,}\) which makes \(g\) not differentiable at this point.

By definition, we know

\begin{align*} g'(0) & = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{g(0+h) - g(0)}{h}\\ & = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{g(h) - g(0)}{h}\\ & = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{|h|} - \sqrt{|0|}}{h}\\ & = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\sqrt{|h|}}{h} \end{align*}
