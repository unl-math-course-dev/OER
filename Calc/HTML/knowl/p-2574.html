Note that \(p''(x)=0\) at \(x=-1\) and \(x=2\text{.}\) The second derivative sign chart for \(p\) shows that \(p''(x)\) is negative for \(-1 \lt x \lt 2\) and positive for all other values of \(x\text{.}\) Since \(p''\) changes sign at \(x = -1\) and \(x = 2\text{,}\) \(p\) has points of inflection at \(x = -1\) and \(x = 2\text{.}\) In particular, \(p\) is concave down for \(-1 \lt x \lt 2\) and concave up for all other values of \(x\text{.}\)
