The value of the numerator at \(x=c\) is \(e^{-c} \ne 0\text{,}\) while the value of the denominator is \(0\) at \(x=c\text{.}\) Thus, \(\lim_{x \to c^+} \frac{e^{-x}}{x-c} = \infty\) and \(\lim_{x \to c^-} \frac{e^{-x}}{x-c} = -\infty\text{.}\) This makes the line \(x=c\) a vertical asymptote for \(q\text{.}\)
