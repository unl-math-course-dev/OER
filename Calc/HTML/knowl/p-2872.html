First, observe that \(p\) is defined for all real numbers by assumption, so \(p(a)\) has some finite value. However, \(p\) is not differentiable at \(x = a\) and \(p\) is concave up to the left of \(x = a\) and concave down to the right of \(x = a\text{.}\) This indicates that \(p\) has a relative maximum at \(x = a\text{.}\) Not enough information is provided, however, to determine if this relative maximum is a global maximum, nor if \(p\) happens to have a global minimum at some other point.
