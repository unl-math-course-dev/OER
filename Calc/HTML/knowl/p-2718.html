As we noted in (a), \(x = m\) is the only critical number of \(E\text{.}\) More over, since \(e\) to any power is always positive, the sign of \(E'(x)\) depends entirely on the expression \(-\frac{2(x-m)}{2s^2} = -\frac{1}{2}(x-m)\text{.}\) This linear function of \(x\) has negative slope, and thus is positive for \(x \lt m\) and negative for \(x \gt m\text{,}\) and thus \(E'(x)\) is positive for \(x \lt m\) and negative for \(x \gt m\text{.}\) Therefore, \(E\) is increasing for \(x \lt m\) and decreasing for \(x \gt m\text{,}\) with a local maximum at \(x = m\text{.}\)
