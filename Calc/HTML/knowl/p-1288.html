To determine whether \(v\) is increasing or decreasing at \(t = \frac{1}{2}\text{,}\) we compute \(v'(\frac{1}{2})\text{.}\) First, \(v'(t) = t^t(\ln(t) + 1) + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-t^2}}\text{.}\) Hence,

\begin{equation*} v'\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{\frac{1}{2}}\left(\ln\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) + 1\right) + \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-(\frac{1}{2})^2}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \((\ln(\frac{1}{2})+1)\) is positive, it's clear that \(v'(\frac{1}{2}) \gt 0\text{,}\) and thus \(v\) is increasing at \(t = \frac{1}{2}\text{.}\)
