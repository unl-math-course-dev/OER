We begin by writing \(325\) as a product with \(25\) as a factor. Then we continue factoring until only a product of primes remains.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 325 \amp= 25\cdot 13\\ \amp= 5 \cdot 5 \cdot 13 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since the prime factorization is unique, it does not matter how we choose to initially factor the number - the end result will be the same (although you may write your answer in a different order).
