SubsectionSubSectioning

This is an example of a subsection with a little mathematics involved.

A prime number is an integer greater than \(1\) that is divisible only by \(1\) and itself. The smallest prime number is \(2\) and the rest are necessarily odd:

\begin{equation*} 2,3,5,7,11,13,17,19,23,\dots \end{equation*}

Any integer greater than \(1\) that is not prime can be uniquely written as a product of primes. When such a number, like \(42\text{,}\) is written as a product, \(42=2\cdot 21\text{,}\) we say that \(2\cdot 21\) is a factorization of \(42\) and that \(2\) and \(21\) are factors. Note that factors divide the number evenly. We can continue to write factors as products until only a product of primes remains.

Therefore the prime factorization of \(42\) is \(2\cdot 3\cdot 7\text{.}\)

Example1

Determine the prime factorization of \(210\text{.}\)

Solution

We begin by writing \(210\) as a product with \(10\) as a factor. Then we continue factoring until only a product of primes remains.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 210\amp =10\cdot 21\\ \amp =2\cdot 5\cdot 3\cdot 7 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Since the prime factorization is unique, it does not matter how we choose to initially factor the number - the end result will be the same (although you may write your answer in a different order).
