Because \(g'(2) = 0\) and \(g'\) is positive for \(1 \lt x \lt 2\) and for \(2 \lt x \lt 3\text{,}\) it must be that \(g'\) is decreasing for \(1 \lt x \lt 2\) and \(g'\) is increasing for \(2 \lt x \lt 3\text{.}\) Since \(g'\) changes from decreasing to increasing at \(x = 2\text{,}\) it follows that \(g'\) has a local minimum at \(x = 2\) and thus \(g''(2) = 0\text{.}\) In addition, \(g''(x)\) must change sign from negative to positive at \(x = 2\text{.}\) In particular, \(g''\) is negative for \(1 \lt x \lt 2\) and positive for \(2 \lt x \lt 3\text{.}\)
