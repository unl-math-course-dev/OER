Observe that \(p''(x) = 6x - 2a\) and thus \(p''(x) = 0\) implies that \(x = \frac{a}{2}\text{.}\) Since \(p''(x)\) is linear with positive slope, we see that \(p''(x)\) changes sign from negative to positive at \(x = \frac{a}{2}\text{,}\) and thus \(p\) is concave down for \(x \lt \frac{a}{2}\) and concave up for \(x \gt \frac{a}{2}\) and \(x = \frac{a}{2}\) is the only inflection point of \(p\text{.}\)
