Item2.1.13.e

We note that \(\ln(2) \approx 0.6931\) and \(\ln(3) \approx 1.0986\text{.}\) Since these values correspond to the values of

\begin{equation*} \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{a^{h}-1}{h} \end{equation*}

when \(a = 2\) and \(a = 3\text{,}\) we conjecture that

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}[2^x] = 2^x \cdot \ln(2) \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \frac{d}{dx}[3^x] = 3^x \cdot \ln(3) \end{equation*}
