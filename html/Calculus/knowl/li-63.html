Item1.1.6.d

We can approximate the jumper's average velocity on the intervals \([4.9,5]\) and \([5,5.1]\) and find the average of these average velocities. This will give us the slope of the secant line connecting the points \((4.9, s(4.9))\) and \((5.1, s(5.1))\) and be a reasonably good approximation to the instantaneous velocity at \(t = 5\text{.}\) Now

\begin{align*} AV_{[4.9,5]} &= \frac{s(5)-s(4.9)}{5-4.9} \approx 21.31\\ AV_{[5,5.1]} &= \frac{s(5.1)-s(5)}{5.1-5} \approx 22.25 \end{align*}

so the jumper's instantaneous velocity at \(t=5\) is about

\begin{equation*} \frac{21.31+22.25}{2} = 21.78 \end{equation*}

feet per second.
