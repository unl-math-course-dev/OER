The first derivative sign chart shows that \(g'(x) \gt 0\) for \(x \lt -4\text{,}\) \(g'(x) \lt 0\) for \(-4 \lt x \lt 1\text{,}\) \(g'(x) \lt 0\) for \(1 \lt x \lt 2\text{,}\) and \(g'(x) \gt 0\) for \(x \gt 2\text{.}\) By the first derivative test, \(g\) has a local maximum at \(x = -4\) and neither a max nor min at \(x = 1\text{.}\) As these are the only two critical numbers, these are the only two locations for possible extremes. (Note: although \(g\) changes from decreasing to increasing at \(x = 2\text{,}\) this is due to a vertical asymptote, and \(g\) does not have a minimum there.)
