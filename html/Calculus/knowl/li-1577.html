Item2.6.12.c

By the rule for the derivative of an invertible function, we have \((h^{-1})'(\frac{\pi}{2} + 1) = \frac{1}{h'\left(\frac{\pi}{2} \right)}\text{.}\) Thus, we first compute \(h'(x)\) and find that \(h'(x) = 1 + \cos(x)\text{.}\) Since \(h'\left(\frac{\pi}{2} \right) = 1 + \cos\left(\frac{\pi}{2} \right) = 1\text{,}\) it follows that \((h^{-1})'(\frac{\pi}{2} + 1) = \frac{1}{1} = 1\text{.}\)
