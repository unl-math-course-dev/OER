In ActivityÂ 4.15, we will construct a list of the basic antiderivatives we know at this time. Those rules will help us antidifferentiate sums and constant multiples of basic functions. For example, since \(-\cos(x)\) is an antiderivative of \(\sin(x)\) and \(\frac{1}{3}x^3\) is an antiderivative of \(x^2\text{,}\) it follows that

\begin{equation*} F(x) = -5\cos(x) - \frac{4}{3}x^3 \end{equation*}

is an antiderivative of \(f(x) = 5\sin(x) - 4x^2\text{,}\) by the sum and constant multiple rules for differentiation.
