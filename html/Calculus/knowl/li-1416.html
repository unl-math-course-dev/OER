Item2.5.8.c

We have observed in (a) and (b) that \(h'(x) = 3\sin^2(x) \cdot \cos(x)\) and \(r'(x) = \cos(x^3) \cdot 3x^2\text{.}\) Because both parts of the product in \(h'(x)\) are periodic functions, \(h'(x)\) is periodic (with period \(2\pi\)). However, \(r'(x)\) is not periodic, since \(3x^2\) grows without bound as \(x\) increases.
