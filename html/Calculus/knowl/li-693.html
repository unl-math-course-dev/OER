We know that \(g(x) = |x|\) is given by the formula \(g(x) = -x\) when \(x \lt 0\) and by \(g(x) = x\) when \(x \ge 0\text{.}\) Each of these pieces of \(g\) is a straight line, so at every point other than the point where they meet, the function \(g\) has a well-defined slope, and thus is differentiable.
