Item4.2.4.a

Since we are using \(4\) subintervals on \([2,5]\text{,}\) it follows that \(\triangle x = \frac{5-2}{4} = 0.75\text{.}\) Thus, \(x_0 = 2\text{,}\) \(x_1 = 2.75\text{,}\) \(x_2 = 3.5\text{,}\) \(x_3 = 4.25\text{,}\) and \(x_4 = 5\text{.}\) The midpoints of the respective subintervals are thus \(\overline{x_1} = \frac{2+2.75}{2} = 2.375\text{,}\) \(\overline{x_2} = 3.125\text{,}\) \(\overline{x_3} = 3.875\text{,}\) and \(\overline{x_4} = 4.625\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{align*} M_4 &= f(\overline{x_1}) \cdot \triangle x + f(\overline{x_2}) \cdot \triangle x + f(\overline{x_3}) \cdot \triangle x + f(\overline{x_4}) \cdot \triangle x\\ &= \left(f(2.375) + f(3.125) + f(3.875) + f(4.625) \right) \cdot (0.75)\\ &= \left(11.125 + 13.375 + 15.625 + 17.875 \right) \cdot (0.75)\\ &= 58 \cdot 0.75\\ &= 43.5 \end{align*}
