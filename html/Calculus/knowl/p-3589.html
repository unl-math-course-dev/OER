For \(\int_{-1}^1 \sqrt{1-x^2} \, dx\text{,}\) we simply observe that this function is the top half of a circle of radius 1, and thus the bounded region is a semicircle of radius 1, thus having an area of \(\frac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Therefore,

\begin{equation*} \int_{-1}^1 \sqrt{1-x^2} \, dx = \frac{\pi}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
