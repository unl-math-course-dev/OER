Item3.3.3.b

The information we are given shows that \(g\) is increasing on \((a,c)\) and decreasing on \((c,b)\text{.}\) So \(g\) has a global maximum value at \(x=c\text{.}\) We know that \(g\) must have a global minimum at either \(x=a\) or \(x=b\text{.}\) The information given does not tell us how much \(g\) increases on \((a,c)\) and decreases on \((c,b)\text{,}\) so we cannot determine the endpoint at which \(g\) attains its global minimum value.
