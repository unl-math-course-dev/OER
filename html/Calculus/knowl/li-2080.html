Item3.3.3.c

In order for \(h(a)=h(b)\) and \(h''(x) \lt 0\) for all \(x\) in \([a,b]\text{,}\) the graph of \(h\) must look something like a parabola that opens down on the interval \([a,b]\text{.}\) Consequently, \(h\) has its global minimum at \(x=a\) and \(x=b\text{,}\) and its global maximum somewhere in \((a,b)\text{,}\) but we do not know the exact point at which \(h\) attains its global maximum.
