Item3.3.2.c

For \(w(x) = a(1-e^{-bx})\) on \([b, 3b]\text{,}\) we first note that \(w'(x) = a(be^{-bx}) = abe^{-bx}\text{.}\) Since \(a\) and \(b\) are both positive and so is \(e^{-bx}\) for every value of \(x\text{,}\) we see that \(w'(x) \gt 0\) for every value of \(x\text{.}\) Because \(w\) is always increasing (specifically on \([b,3b]\)), \(w\) has its global minimum at the left endpoint (\(g(b) = a(1-e^{-b^2})\)) and its global maximum at the right endpoint (\(g(3b) = a(1-e^{-3b^2})\)).
