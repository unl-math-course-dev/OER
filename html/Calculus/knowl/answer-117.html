Answer3.8.1

  1. \(L\) is an always increasing function.

  2. \(L\) is concave up for all \(t \lt -\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right)\) and concave up for all other values of \(t\text{.}\)

  3. \(\lim_{t \to \infty} \frac{A}{1+ce^{-kt}} = A\text{,}\) and

    \begin{equation*} \lim_{t \to \infty} \frac{A}{1+ce^{-kt}} = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

  4. The inflection point on the graph of \(L\) is \(( -\frac{1}{k} \ln \left(\frac{1}{c}\right), \frac{A}{2})\text{.}\)
