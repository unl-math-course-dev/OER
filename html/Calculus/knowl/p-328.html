Finally, we verify these approximations using an algebraic approach. The definition of the derivative of \(f\) at \(x=a\) shows that

\begin{align*} f'(2) &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(2+h)-f(2)}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\left[(2+h)^2 - 3(2+h)\right] - (-2)}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{\left[4+4h+h^2 - 6 - 3h\right] + 2}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{h^2 + h}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{h(h+1)}{h}\\ &= \lim_{h \to 0} h+1\\ &= 1 \end{align*}

This confirms our conclusions using the numeric and geometric approaches.
