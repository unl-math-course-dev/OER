Exercise5

A moving particle has its velocity given by the quadratic function \(v\) pictured in Figure 5.7. In addition, it is given that \(A_1 = \frac{7}{6}\) and \(A_2 = \frac{8}{3}\text{,}\) as well as that for the corresponding position function \(s\text{,}\) \(s(0) = 0.5\text{.}\)

  1. Use the given information to determine \(s(1)\text{,}\) \(s(3)\text{,}\) \(s(5)\text{,}\) and \(s(6)\text{.}\)

  2. On what interval(s) is \(s\) increasing? On what interval(s) is \(s\) decreasing?

  3. On what interval(s) is \(s\) concave up? On what interval(s) is \(s\) concave down?

  4. Sketch an accurate, labeled graph of \(s\) on the axes at right in Figure 5.7.

  5. Note that \(v(t) = -2 + \frac{1}{2}(t-3)^2\text{.}\) Find a formula for \(s\text{.}\)
