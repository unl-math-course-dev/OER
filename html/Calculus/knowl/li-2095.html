Item3.3.4.b

From our work in (a), we know that \(s\) has critical numbers at \(t = \frac{5\pi}{12} \pm \frac{k\pi}{2}\text{.}\) On the interval \([0, \frac{\pi}{2}]\text{,}\) only \(t = \frac{5\pi}{12}\) lies in the interval. Thus we have three values at which to evaluate \(s\text{.}\)

Doing so:

  • \(s(\frac{5\pi}{12}) = 8\text{;}\)

  • \(s(0) = 3\sin(2(0-\frac{\pi}{6})) + 5 = 3\sin(-\frac{\pi}{3}) + 5 = 5 - \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 2.402\text{;}\) and

  • \(s(\frac{\pi}{2}) = 3\sin(2(\frac{\pi}{2}-\frac{\pi}{6})) + 5 = 3\sin(\frac{2\pi}{3}) + 5 = 5 + \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 7.598\text{.}\)

The global max of \(s\) on \([0, \frac{\pi}{2}]\) is thus \(s(\frac{5\pi}{12}) = 8\text{,}\) while the global min is \(s(0) = 5 - \frac{3\sqrt{3}}{2} \approx 2.402\text{.}\)
